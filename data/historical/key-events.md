# Key events — the monetary and housing-financial timeline

Each entry is a load-bearing event in the structure described across this repo. Dates are when the event takes effect, not necessarily when it was announced.

## The monetary substrate

| Year | Event | Why it matters |
|------|-------|---------------|
| 1944 | Bretton Woods system established | USD pegged to gold; other currencies pegged to USD. Monetary expansion externally constrained. |
| 1971 | Nixon ends USD-gold convertibility | All major currencies become pure fiat. No external anchor on money supply. The denominator of "house price" becomes elastic. |
| 1974 | Saudi-US petrodollar agreement | Oil priced and settled in USD globally. Creates permanent global demand for USD; recycles oil surpluses into US bank deposits and Treasuries — the structural conduit for capital into US (and Anglosphere) real estate. |
| 1989 | Japanese asset price bubble peaks | Nikkei + Tokyo land at all-time highs. The subsequent 30-year deflation is the canonical example of a credit-housing loop reversing in a high-fiat-creation system. |
| 1999 | Glass-Steagall partially repealed (Gramm-Leach-Bliley) | Commercial and investment banking re-merged in the US. Enables the bank-as-everything model that becomes the 2008 conduit. |
| 2008 | US subprime mortgage crisis | The derivative stack ([analysis/06-financialization.md](../../analysis/06-financialization.md)) becomes load-bearing on housing fundamentals and snaps. ~$8T in global household wealth erased. |
| 2008–2014 | Fed QE1, QE2, QE3 | Fed buys $1.7T of MBS by 2014. Central bank becomes structural backstop for the mortgage market — a new permanent feature, not an emergency measure. |
| 2017 | Toronto foreign buyer tax + Vancouver Empty Homes Tax | First major Anglo policy attempts to address the empty-asset-unit phenomenon. |
| 2020 Mar | COVID emergency Fed QE | M2 +25% in 18 months. US home prices +40% in same window. The cleanest natural experiment showing housing prices respond to money supply, not housing fundamentals. |
| 2022 Mar | Fed begins QT and rate hikes | First sustained reversal of post-2008 monetary regime. Housing market begins correction. |
| 2023 Oct | Sitzer-Burnett verdict against NAR ($1.8B) | First serious legal challenge to the US realtor commission structure. |
| 2024 Mar | NAR settlement | Buyer-agent commissions formally unbundled in the US. Behavior change unfolding through 2025–26. |
| 2025+ | Mortgage rates persistently high; BRICS settlement experiments | The post-petrodollar transition (slow, partial) is now a measurable financial variable. Anglo housing markets begin to lose the structural USD-capital bid. |

## What this timeline shows

Three structural shifts are visible:

1. **1971 → 1974**: the unit of account becomes elastic, then globally USD-denominated. This is the pre-condition for everything that follows.
2. **1999 → 2008**: financial deregulation lets the derivative stack reach a height the underlying housing can't support.
3. **2008 → 2026**: central-bank backstopping makes the system *survive* its own contradictions, but at the cost of permanent housing inflation and permanent monetary expansion.

Each phase compounds the previous. We are currently entering a possible fourth phase (post-petrodollar, post-zero-rate) whose shape isn't yet clear.
