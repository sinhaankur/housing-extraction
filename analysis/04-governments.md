# 4. Governments — the fourth tier

*Placeholder for expansion. The source brief states "you already understand that part" — fill in with:*

- **Construction-stage taxes**: HST/GST, development charges, permit fees, density / community-benefit charges
- **First-sale taxes**: HST on new builds, land transfer tax
- **Resale taxes**: land transfer tax (municipal + provincial in cities like Toronto), capital gains where applicable
- **Holding taxes**: property tax, vacancy tax, foreign-buyer surcharges, speculation taxes
- **Indirect**: tax revenue on every player above (developer corporate tax, agent income tax, bank corporate tax)

For the Toronto $700k example in [case-studies/toronto-condo-700k.md](../case-studies/toronto-condo-700k.md), governments capture **~$80,000–110,000 cumulatively across players** at the construction + first-sale stage alone, then another **~$22,000** in land transfer tax at the first resale (Toronto municipal LTT + Ontario provincial LTT combined).

## Why this layer is the most defensible (politically)

Unlike the other three extractors, the government tier:
- Operates with statutory authority
- Notionally returns value to public goods
- But functionally creates a **strong fiscal incentive to keep transaction volume high and prices appreciating**, which aligns government interests with the *other* extractors and against affordability
