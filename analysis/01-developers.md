# 1. Developers — the largest single profit slice, embedded in the very first sale

Developers are the only player who gets paid **once per unit ever built**, but they take the biggest single cut on that first transaction.

## Headline margins

### Canada (Toronto pre-construction condos)
- Developers typically run a **12–20% profit margin** on a typical Toronto condo project. [Toronto Realty Blog]
- Reasonable expectation: **10–20%** of finished product, sometimes up to **25%**. [GTA Homes]
- Publicly traded builders: **20–25% gross margins**. A $1M condo throws off $200–250k gross. [Quora]
- Per-square-foot breakdown (downtown Toronto): cost ~$665–775/sqft against $900/sqft sale price → developer keeps **$125–235/sqft**. [GTA Homes]

### International benchmark
- Gross development profit margin is **15–25%** — global industry standard taught in real estate finance textbooks. [Getrefm]
- Typical project: ingredients cost 80, finished product sells for 100. [Getrefm]

### Country snapshots
- **India** (less transparent): listed players (DLF, Godrej, Lodha, Prestige) report **25–35% gross margins** on residential; net margins after financing run **12–18%**.
- **US, Australia, UK**: cluster in the same **15–25%** band.

## How developers amplify those headline margins

The published margin understates actual capture. Four mechanisms:

### Pre-sales / off-plan sales
Sell units 2–4 years before completion. Lock in today's price. Use **buyer deposits as construction working capital**.
- Banks won't extend construction financing until pre-sales commit demand. [LendCity Mortgages]
- Buyer takes the construction risk. Developer takes the upside.

### Price escalation between launch phases
A 40-storey tower sells floors 1–10 at a "launch price," then 11–20 at +5%, then 21–30 at another +5%.
- Not driven by cost increases. Pure price discovery against committed demand.

### Assignment sales (shadow flipping)
Investors who bought pre-construction sell their contract before the building completes.
- Developer charges an **assignment fee of $5,000–$25,000** per resale.
- Canada Revenue Agency is analyzing **2,810 transactions** involving pre-construction condo flipping in Toronto. [CBC]
- A separate revenue stream the developer earns just for *permitting* the resale.

### Parking, locker, and upgrade sales
- Margins often **50–100%**.
- A $50,000 parking spot might cost $15,000 to actually build.

## Why this matters

Developers are the only extractor who must add something physical to get paid. After that first sale, they exit — but the unit they built becomes a permanent revenue-generating asset for the *other* three extractor classes (agents, banks, governments), in perpetuity.
