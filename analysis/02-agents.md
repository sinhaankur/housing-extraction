# 2. Real estate agents — the recurring extractor on every resale

Agents are paid **every time** a unit changes hands. A condo that sells four times in 60 years pays four sets of commissions on the same physical asset.

The **North American commission system is a global outlier** — and it's the mechanism that makes "every sale gets taxed again" feel especially extractive, because it's not just government taxes; it's also the agents.

| Netherlands   | 1–2%             | Seller                                            |

## Why the US system is a global anomaly

The Richmond Federal Reserve called this out explicitly: the level of commission — especially the **buyer-agent commission** — makes the US an anomaly.
- In the UK, Ireland, Netherlands, Singapore, Sweden, and Norway, sellers typically pay **<2%**. [Richmond Fed]
- In Australia, Canada, and Denmark, buyers commonly purchase without agent representation.

## The economic puzzle

Every other intermediary industry (stock brokers, travel agents, insurance) saw commissions collapse with the internet. Real estate in the US and Canada **did not**.

**Why:** the buyer's agent commission is paid by the seller, but recommended to the buyer *by their own agent*. If a buyer's agent steers their client away from properties offering low commissions, the system polices itself. Agents protect agents.

In the US this triggered the **Sitzer–Burnett antitrust lawsuit (2023)** — $1.8B verdict against the National Association of Realtors — and the March 2024 settlement that supposedly unbundled commissions. Whether behavior actually changed is still being debated.

## The dollar value of agent extraction over time

A Toronto condo selling at $700,000 generates **~$35,000 in commissions per transaction**.

If it sells four times across its 60-year useful life: **$140,000 in agent fees on a single physical unit** — more than the unit cost to build in 1995.

The agents in 2030 add no value the original 1995 builder didn't already produce. They just match buyer and seller.

## Aggregate scale

US real estate agents collect **~$75 billion per year** in commissions. That's the entire GDP of Kenya or Bulgaria — paid annually to people whose primary function is matchmaking on transactions that wouldn't exist without sellers and buyers already wanting to transact. [Mortgagecalculator]
